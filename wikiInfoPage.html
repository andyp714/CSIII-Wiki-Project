JOE DART/

Thanks to his tasteful chops, funky grooves, rapid finger work, and unbelievable pocket, Joe Dart from Vulfpeck is one of the most interesting bassist on the modern scene.
Joe Dart first picked up a bass at the age of eight, when his parents suggested that he and his siblings form a family band. The family band never materialized, but Dart stuck with it, and soon developed a love for the instrument, and for the bass-driven funk and soul music of the 60's and 70's. Influenced early-on by players such as Flea, Verdine White, Rocco Prestia and Pino Palladino, Dart found the opportunity to hone his chops and his ear in high school, both in his school's music program and with his regularly gigging band. [source]

VULFPECK/

Joe Dart is the bassist for the American funk band Vulfpeck. Joe Dart, Jack Stratton, Theo Katzman, and Woody Goss founded the band in 2011. Over their career, Vulfpeck has released four EPs, six studio album (listed below) and one live album. Vulfpeck publishes their music through their own label titled VULF RECORDS. They are the first band to sell out Madison Square Garden without a manager or backing label. [source]

BACKGROUND/

The founding band members all attended the University of Michigan's music school. They first collaborated together for a recording at the University of Michigan. Their earliest works, such as Beastly, were realeased on YouTube. [source]
